# Leave Policy - FTE_CN_GZ

Document ID: LP-FTE-CN-GZ-2026
Applies To: Full-time employees (FTE) in Guangzhou, China
Effective Date: January 1, 2026

## 0. Scope and Rule Hierarchy
This document defines annual leave and sick leave request/approval workflow.
For org and grade definitions, refer to:
- "Org & Grade Framework - FTE_CN_GZ"
If any conflict exists, the stricter rule applies.

## A. Annual Leave

### A1. Leave Unit
Annual leave can be requested in increments of 0.5 day.
Requests below 0.5 day are not accepted in the HR leave system.

### A2. Advance Notice
For planned annual leave of 1 to 3 days, employees should submit at least 2 calendar days in advance.
For planned annual leave of more than 3 days, employees should submit at least 7 calendar days in advance.
If there is an urgent personal reason, manager may approve an exception with written note.

### A3. Maximum Consecutive Days
Employees can take up to 10 consecutive annual leave days in one request.
Any request longer than 10 consecutive days requires HRBP review and Director-level approval.

### A4. Approval Rules
- 0.5 to 2 days: direct manager approval is required.
- More than 2 days up to 5 days: direct manager plus skip-level manager approval is required.
- More than 5 days up to 10 days: direct manager, skip-level manager, and HRBP approval are required.
- Over 10 days: handled as an exception case, requiring HRBP and Director approval.

### A5. Carry-over Note
Unused annual leave carry-over is governed by local labor law and separate payroll policy.
This document only defines request and approval workflow.

## B. Sick Leave

### B1. Leave Unit
Sick leave can be requested in increments of 0.5 day.

### B2. Notification Timeline
Employee should notify direct manager as soon as reasonably possible on the same day.
If the employee cannot notify directly, a delegated contact is acceptable.

### B3. Medical Certificate Requirement
For sick leave longer than or equal to 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate is required.
For repeated sick leave requests totaling 4 days within 30 calendar days, HR may request additional medical proof.

### B4. Accepted Certificate Format
The uploaded document should include all of the following:
- Employee legal name (or national ID tail number)
- Visit date
- Diagnosis or clinical recommendation for rest
- Recommended rest duration
- Hospital or clinic name
- Physician signature or official stamp

Preferred file format:
- PDF, JPG, or PNG
- Maximum 10 MB per file
- One request can include up to 3 attachments

### B5. Approval Rules
- 0.5 to 2 days: direct manager approval.
- More than 2 days up to 5 days: direct manager plus skip-level manager approval.
- More than 5 days: direct manager, skip-level manager, and HRBP approval.
- Any request missing required certificate cannot move to final approval.

### B6. Exception Handling
In emergency hospitalization scenarios where documents are delayed, manager may place the request in provisional pending state for up to 5 business days.
If documents are not provided within that period, the case should be returned for correction.
